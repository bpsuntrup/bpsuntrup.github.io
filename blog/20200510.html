    Saturday routine

    I should have a Saturday routine. It should start after breakfast.

    I have a lot of work to do. I want to grow my own food.

    I've decided that most of my food should be based on animals. I've tried eating plants. Plants suck (as food). Plants are what my food eats.

    Some plants are good though. I want to grow greens, carrots, potatoes, onions, and tomatoes. I think that's probably enough plants.

    The rest of my food will be chickens, chicken eggs, rabbits, and maybe swine. I'd like to grow some of my own red meat. I'm not good at hunting. If I knew for a fact that I would be able to kill a deer every year, I might not worry about breeding red meat. I'd like a flock of goats. eventually.

    Maybe even sheep.

    Or maybe sheep and goats.

    I have to mow grass in the summer. That should be a part of my routine too. I like to do that on Friday, though.

    A Saturday routine (for starters) doesn't have to be all-encompassing. The most important thing is having some kind of structure to the day. I tend to feel totally lost without day structure.

    I should probably keep a to do list of things to... do around the farm. Then part of my routine can be grooming the to do list.

    I should probably check the mail on Saturdays.

    I should probably balance the finances on Saturdays.

    I should probably count my ammo, clean my assault rifles, sharpen my Bowie knives, and re-apply poison to my darts on Saturdays. Also a good day to check all my locks and tune my chainsaw booby traps.

    I'll start with the following Saturday routine after breakfast:

    • balance finances
    • groom to do list
    • get mail
